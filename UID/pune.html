Pune - The Oxford of the East

About Pune

Pune, located in the state of Maharashtra, is often referred to as the "Oxford of the East" due to its numerous educational institutions and vibrant academic atmosphere. It is also known for its rich history and cultural heritage.

The city is an emerging IT hub, with a thriving industrial and automotive sector. Pune is known for its pleasant climate, historical landmarks, and a blend of modern and traditional lifestyles.

Discover Pune

Fun Facts About Pune

1. Pune is home to the prestigious Fergusson College, one of the oldest educational institutions in India, established in 1885.

2. The city is famous for the Aga Khan Palace, a historic landmark where Mahatma Gandhi was imprisoned during the Indian freedom struggle.

3. Pune's Osho International Meditation Resort attracts visitors from around the world for spiritual and wellness retreats.

4. The city hosts the popular Pune International Film Festival, showcasing films from various countries and promoting cultural exchange.

Explore Pune

Historical Sites

Explore Pune's historical sites, including Shaniwar Wada, Sinhagad Fort, and the Pataleshwar Cave Temple, which offer a glimpse into the city's rich past.

Colonial Architecture

Admire Pune's colonial architecture, with buildings like the Pune University, built in the Gothic style, adding to the city's charm.

Festivals and Events

Experience Pune's vibrant festivals and events, such as Ganesh Chaturthi, Pune Festival, and classical music concerts at the Sawai Gandharva Bhimsen Mahotsav.

Food and Dining

Indulge in Pune's diverse culinary scene, offering everything from traditional Maharashtrian dishes to international cuisines and trendy cafes.
