Kolkata - The City of Joy

About Kolkata

Kolkata, the capital of West Bengal, is famously known as the "City of Joy". It is a city rich in culture, history, and tradition, serving as the cultural and intellectual hub of India.

The city is known for its colonial architecture, art galleries, and literary heritage. Kolkata is also the birthplace of several notable poets, writers, and artists, contributing significantly to Indian culture.

Discover Kolkata

Fun Facts About Kolkata

1. Kolkata is home to the largest book fair in Asia, the Kolkata Book Fair, attracting millions of book lovers every year.

2. The city boasts the iconic Howrah Bridge, one of the longest cantilever bridges in the world.

3. Kolkata is famous for its festivals, particularly Durga Puja, which is celebrated with grand festivities and elaborate decorations.

4. The city has a rich literary heritage, being the birthplace of Rabindranath Tagore, the first Asian Nobel laureate in Literature.

Explore Kolkata

Colonial Architecture

Discover Kolkata's colonial architecture, with landmarks such as Victoria Memorial, St. Paul's Cathedral, and the Writer's Building.

Art and Culture

Immerse yourself in the city's art and culture scene, with numerous galleries, theaters, and cultural events showcasing its rich heritage.

Festivals

Experience the vibrant festivals of Kolkata, especially Durga Puja, which is celebrated with immense joy and grandeur across the city.

Culinary Delights

Indulge in Kolkata's culinary delights, known for its sweet delicacies like rosogolla and sandesh, as well as street food like kathi rolls and puchkas.
