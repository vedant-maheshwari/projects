Lucknow - The City of Nawabs

About Lucknow

Lucknow, the capital city of Uttar Pradesh, is renowned for its rich cultural heritage, historical landmarks, and exquisite cuisine. Known as the "City of Nawabs", it reflects the grandeur and elegance of the bygone era.

The city is a significant cultural and artistic hub of North India, with a history that is deeply intertwined with the Mughal and British eras, evident in its architecture and traditions.

Discover Lucknow

Fun Facts About Lucknow

1. Lucknow is famous for its refined manners, beautiful gardens, and exquisite cuisine, particularly kebabs and biryanis.

2. The city is home to the grand Bara Imambara, an architectural marvel built in 1784 by Nawab Asaf-ud-Daula.

3. Lucknow's cultural heritage is reflected in its classical music, dance forms, and poetry, especially Urdu literature.

4. The city is a prominent center for Chikan embroidery, a traditional craft that is highly valued for its intricate patterns and craftsmanship.

Explore Lucknow

Historical Monuments

Explore Lucknow's historical monuments, including the Rumi Darwaza, Chota Imambara, and the Residency, which tell the story of the city's rich past.

Mughal Architecture

Admire the stunning Mughal architecture, with its intricate details and grandeur, that is prominent throughout the city.

Cultural Events

Participate in Lucknow's cultural events and festivals, which showcase its rich traditions in music, dance, and cuisine.

Culinary Experiences

Indulge in Lucknow's culinary experiences, renowned for its Awadhi cuisine, which includes dishes like kebabs, biryanis, and sweet delicacies.
